The court proceedings and trials dataset

This page describes the Court Proceedings Datasets. The set consists of 10 CSV files, with each line providing information about a magistrates court proceeding, or a crown court trial. Only cases with adult defendants are included.

Caveats

  • It is important to note that these data have been extracted from large administrative data systems generated by the courts and police forces. As a consequence, care should be taken to ensure data collection processes and their inevitable limitations are taken into account when those data are used.
  • The dataset includes data a principal offence basis only. When a defendant has been found guilty of two or more offences the principal offence is the offence for which the heaviest penalty is imposed. Where the same disposal is imposed for two or more offences, the offence selected is the offence for which the statutory maximum penalty is the most severe.

Please also note that the description below is currently incomplete. We are working to improve it.

Example

The following line:

1,0,0,0,0,MC,IND,33,51,01,2002,2048,1,1,00806,0,01,860,000,000,000,2,070,0,0,03,01,00008,1

is decoded as:

  • 1 defendant, 51 year-old defendant, male, white
  • Magistrates court
  • Indictable offense
  • Location: Leicestershire police force / Leicester local justice area
  • Happened: January 2002
  • Offense: Assault occasioning actual bodily harm
  • Primary disposal: No evidence offered/not proceeded against/charge withdrawn
  • Triable either way (non motoring)
  • Defendant not found guilty, and not sentenced
  • Result: withdrawn
  • HO/ONS Offence type: Violence against the person
  • Notifiable offense
